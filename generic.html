About Marshall

Zookeeper turned analyst

From cleaning seal teeth to cleaning data

Hi, I'm Marshall. The past five years I've worked at two prestigious zoos caring and training for animals such as seals, otters, snow leopards, lions, beavers, ravens, and more. I enjoyed every day at work, even the ones where I spent an hour cleaning after three otter pups, left for two minutes, and returned to find an even worse mess than before.

So why analytics? If there is one word that best describes me, it is curious. I love to learn and I can't help myself deep diving into various topics. Does hosting the Olympics really help those cities in the long term? How will the new pitch clock rule affect baseball games? What does the cost savings analysis look like for installing solar panels to a single family home rooftop?

Anyone can write an opinion piece on these topics, but the ones that hold up use data to support their claim. These metrics come from digital platforms or databases of some kind. I imagine I get the same rush from discovering insights as miners when discover gold. From here, we can find trends, correlations, and other learnings to form conclusions in a satisfying way. Additionally, this can be done without smelling like I just prepared 200lbs worth of fish for marine mammal diets.

Once I made the decision to switch career paths I focused on studying anything data related. In doing so, I've become credentialed in the following:

  • Google Data Analytics
  • Google Analytics Individual Qualification
  • Google Ads Search
  • Google Ads Display
  • Programming Languages such as SQL, R, Python

Whether you are a hiring manager, fellow data enthusiast, or random passerby, let's connect on LinkedIn!

Outside of work, I enjoy playing volleyball, reading fantasy series that are a probably too long, playing guitar, watching the Washington Capitals, and bird watching. Currently located in Chicago.
